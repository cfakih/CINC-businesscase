Executive Summary

The Salesloft security breach in August prompted a comprehensive reevaluation of your sales engagement platform. While the integration has since been restored, the evaluation revealed fundamental gaps in Salesloft's ability to support CINC's growth trajectory across your entire 87-person revenue organization.

🎯 Critical Decision Timeline

Decision needs to be made fast to allow time for implementation before holidays and Q1. Your busiest quarter requires a platform that's already live and optimized. The MVP launch approach protects revenue execution while enabling January 1 platform adoption.

Current Reality Assessment

After seven comprehensive demos across all business units, Crysta's assessment: "We know what we want and we know what we need and we know what we have that isn't serving us."

Core Issue: Salesloft functions only as a sales engagement layer. While it can provide sequences for Client Services, it won't give them a unified experience to look at the whole journey from SDR-AE-AM in a unified, seamless, intuitive way. It provides no deal management, offers limited conversation intelligence, and lacks flexibility for your unique B2B motion (selling to real estate agents who operate like individual businesses). Meanwhile, your CS team is "embarrassingly" manual (Danielle's words), with account managers "pinging through dials all day long with not a lot of structure."

8 Critical Gaps Leading to Organizational Inefficiencies

1. Bidirectional Sync Failure

Manual imports "a few times a day" creating black holes in reporting and delayed sequence triggers. This architectural limitation forces reps to manually import Salesforce changes multiple times daily, leading to data inconsistencies and reporting gaps.

2. Pipeline Management Paralysis

Hundreds of opportunities stuck in "evaluating" stage because managers must manually update opportunities one-by-one in Salesforce. This creates forecast accuracy issues beyond monthly view and forces leadership to avoid pipeline hygiene due to time cost.

3. Client Services Operating in the Dark Age

60-person organization entirely manual - spreadsheets, template filling, zero automation. Implementation team manually filling Salesforce templates and copying Zoom AI summaries one-by-one. Account management team "pinging through dials all day long with not a lot of structure."

4. Reporting & Analytics Deficiency

"Triangulating" Salesforce + Salesloft data with no drillable insights. Cannot answer which reps drive sequence results, which sequence steps generate bookings, or provide rep-to-rep performance comparison.

5. Conversation Intelligence Underutilization

80% of demos recorded but only "light coaching... skim transcripts." Missing keyword search across all calls, call libraries/playlists for training, and lifecycle visibility across departments.

6. Real Estate Agent Workflow Friction

Salesloft's rigid structure doesn't accommodate your unique B2B motion. Opportunity grid defaults to account name (irrelevant for CINC), forcing workflows that don't match your sales process.

7. Lead Conversion Activity Loss

Historical activity disappears when lead converts to opportunity. AEs lack context, require re-discovery calls, leading to duplicated discovery efforts and slower deal velocity.

8. Vendor Relationship Deterioration

Multiple unanswered inquiries about Drift cancellation and renewal terms. "Forced Rhythm on us... took months of tickets, never got on face-to-face call." Uncertainty around February renewal and inability to plan technology roadmap.

Strategic Opportunity

This isn't about escaping a failing vendor—it's about recognizing that CINC has outgrown what Salesloft can deliver. Your sales team needs true bidirectional sync, drillable reporting, and deal management. Your Client Services organization needs automation and structure.
